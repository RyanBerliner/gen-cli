A rational person should assume that human understanding has future value. If
you don't believe this, you will be inclined to bypass understanding whenever
possible. If the future doesn't deliver, you'll be useless and dependent.

So it stands to think you must assume no dystopian future exists, and instead
seek tools that **increase** your human understanding. This is a form of
[pascals wager.][0]

LLM tooling for software development has begun migrating its users competence
to the cloud. You should be able to reap the benefits of LLMs for software
development without fighting your tools temptations to become clueless.

THIS MEANS THAT LLM TOOLING MUST:

1. NEVER accumulate ignorance or error. The most simple violation of this
   rule is tools which loop, where a decision is made based on a previously
   unknown and/or possibly incorrect decision[^1].

2. NEVER be self-destructive. Consider a nail gun which shoots decreasingly
   opaque nails. After some time the operator would no longer be able to judge
   the tools effectiveness or impact.

3. AVOID making the user wait. Understanding a piece of software requires deep
   and continual focus. Waiting interrupts focus, and in the extreme it
   encourages context switching. This destroys focus altogether.


[^1]: Its worth acknowledging that recursive decisions (loops) also describe
    the token generation process itself. The irony is not lost on me.

[0]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascal's_wager
